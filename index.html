The Shift: Checklist vs. Mindset

The traditional NEST process provided a robust technical checklist, but it often left technicians feeling like "robots" and customers feeling processed. NEST 2.0, powered by the G.O.P.R.O. engine, shifts the focus to human psychology. We aren't just checking boxes; we are managing anxiety and building authority.

Old NEST

  • Focus on "Interviewing" the client
  • 5-Tier Options (Decision Fatigue)
  • Technical "Load Calculations" focus
  • Objection Handling at the end

NEST 2.0 (G.O.P.R.O.)

  • Focus on "Leading" via G.A.C.A.
  • 3-Option "Sweet Spot" (Good/Better/Best)
  • "Symptom vs. System" focus
  • Objection Discovery throughout

Step 1: Nurture (The First 60 Seconds)

Trust Velocity: Speed to Rapport

In the old model, technicians attempted to build trust slowly throughout the call. In the NEST 2.0 model, we front-load trust using G.A.C.A. This creates a "Trust Reservoir" that sustains the relationship through the Evaluation and Solutions phases, preventing the dip that usually occurs when price is mentioned.

Step 3: Solutions (The Rule of Three)

The original NEST PDF suggested 5 star-tiered packages. While comprehensive, psychology shows that excessive choice leads to "Analysis Paralysis." NEST 2.0 simplifies this to Good, Better, Best. This "Goldilocks Effect" dramatically increases the likelihood of a customer choosing the middle (Better) option rather than choosing nothing at all.

Option 1: Good

Fixes the immediate symptom. "Stops the bleeding."

Option 2: Better (The Sweet Spot)

Fixes the symptom + protects the system. Most popular choice.

Option 3: Best

Total restoration. The "Like New" permanent solution.
